My Bookshelf

The Past, Present, and Future of my Literary Consumption

Want to Read

Tribe of Mentors.

NYT best-selling author, Tim Ferriss, distilled the wisdom from thousands of hours of podcast interviews with titans of industry, academia, and life in general in his most recent book, Tools of Titans. For his upcoming book, he compiled a list of his most effective questions and solicited responses to those questions via email from his interviewee "reach list," which included, Neil Gaiman, Ayaan Hursi Ali, and the Dalai Lama. Tribe of Mentors is the compilation of the 140 respondents, which includes the likes of Ben Stiller, Yuval Noah Harari, and Terry Crews.

Tim's work, primarily Tools of Titans in conjunction with his podcast, have helped improve my life immeasurably in 2017. Journaling, meditating, dieting, and exercising are practices which I started doing with fanatic regularity after becoming a follower of Tim's work. I will definitely be picking up a copy of Tribe of Mentors come November.

Touchstones

Books that changed me.

On Writing: A Memoir of the Craft

Steven King

A must-read for anyone engaged in creative endeavors. This is a pithy, punchy, and powerful read with nuggets of wisdom which buoy me in moments of doubt.

Radical Acceptance

Tara Brach, PhD

A heartfelt account of the power of meditation. This book spurred my daily meditation practice (I use Headspace).

The Omnivore's Dilemma

Michael Pollan

An eye-opening survey of the sources of our food. This book made me a more conscious consumer and challenged me to be more mindful of my food choices.
